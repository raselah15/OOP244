In this workshop, I learned how to design and enhance a class by implementing helper functions, operator overloads, and proper stream handling in C++. One of the most important concepts I strengthened was the difference between member functions, non-member helper functions, and friend functions. I better understand why friend functions should generally be avoided in object-oriented design because they break encapsulation, but I also see why they are sometimes necessary, such as when directly accessing private data for specific operator overloads.

I also learned how to properly overload insertion (<<) and extraction (>>) operators, including how to implement foolproof input validation. Handling invalid input without crashing the program was challenging at first, especially managing stream states, clearing errors, and ignoring leftover characters. Understanding how formatting manipulators like setw, setprecision, setfill, and alignment flags affect output formatting was another key learning experience. Matching the exact required output formatting required careful attention to detail.

One difficulty I encountered was correctly implementing the GPA calculation and grade mapping to match the expected output. Small mistakes in rounding or formatting caused noticeable differences. Debugging those formatting issues took time but helped me better understand how streams behave.

Overall, this workshop significantly improved my understanding of operator overloading, stream manipulation, and maintaining proper object-oriented structure. I think providing a visual breakdown of the grade and GPA mapping earlier in the workshop could help students avoid confusion. Otherwise, the workshop was very useful and practical for strengthening C++ fundamentals.
